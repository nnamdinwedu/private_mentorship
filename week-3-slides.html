WEEK 3:

Zones of Interest

Mastering the Areas Where Smart Money Makes Decisions

Lesson Objective

By the end of this week, you will be able to:

  • Identify high-probability Supply & Demand Zones.
  • Distinguish between Internal and External Structure.
  • Accurately mark Fair Value Gaps (FVGs).
  • Refine your entry zones for tighter risk and higher reward.

1. Understanding Supply & Demand

What Are Points of Interest (POIs)?

These are areas where institutions and Smart Money have left their footprints.

These zones are the most important areas on your chart. Most setups fail because traders use imprecise points.

Your Goal:

Learn to identify these points precisely so you can trade with them, not against them.

Valid Zones Explained

Valid Supply Zones (for Sells)

  • The origin of a strong bearish move.
  • Must have broken structure (BOS).
  • Should be unmitigated or partially mitigated.

Valid Demand Zones (for Buys)

  • The origin of a strong bullish move.
  • Must have broken structure (BOS).
  • Often found after a liquidity grab.

We’ll teach you to mark zones on the higher timeframe where the structure broke, then refine on a lower timeframe for a perfect entry.

2. Internal vs. External Structure

There are swings within swings. It’s critical to know the difference.

Internal Structure

The small micro-swings within a larger trend.

Think of it as the skin of the market. Useful for refining entries, but not for confirming the overall trend.

External Structure

The major swing points that define the overall trend (BOS or CHoCH on higher timeframes).

This is the market's skeleton. It confirms your directional bias.

3. Fair Value Gaps (FVGs)

FVGs are market inefficiencies that institutions will often return to fill.

What to look for:

  • A 3-candle structure where the middle candle’s body doesn't overlap with the wicks of the candles on either side.
  • They represent a large, fast move where price left a gap.

Mitigation

When price returns to an FVG, it is considered mitigated. We want to find unmitigated FVGs for our entries.

4. Pinpointing Entry Zones

We use a combination of these concepts for a razor-sharp entry.

Confirmed Structure Displacement (CSD)

The candle that caused the BOS. Price often returns to this area for a clean entry.

FVGs as Precision Entries

We use the midpoint of an FVG (the Consequent Encroachment) for a sniper entry, especially when combined with an inducement sweep.

Your entry is a calculated probability, not luck.
