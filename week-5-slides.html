WEEK 5:

Execution & Risk Management

Protect Capital. Let Profits Run.

Lesson Objective

By the end of this week, you will master how to:

  • Enforce your fixed 1:3 Risk-to-Reward rule.
  • Manage trades without micro-managing.
  • Make smart pre-weekend decisions.
  • Track and evaluate trades using a professional journal.

1. Enforcing Your 1:3 RR Rule

This rule is non-negotiable for long-term success.

Why 1:3?

  • You only need to win 3 out of 10 trades to be profitable.
  • It trains emotional detachment—you don't need every trade to win.
  • It forces you to let trades breathe and hit your target.

"When you aim for a 1:3 RR, you’re no longer chasing wins — you’re playing the long game."

2. Trade Management

Knowing what to do after you enter a trade is an art.

When to Break Even (BE):

Move your stop loss to break even ONLY when there is high-impact news approaching. Otherwise, let the trade run.

Moving to BE prematurely can often get you stopped out just before the trade moves in your favor. Patience is key.

When to Scale In:

Only when you are already risk-free on the original position and a new confirmation appears.

3. Weekend Trade Rules

This is where many traders give back their week's profits. We won't.

Rule #1: Close your trades UNLESS...

  • The trade is in strong profit (1R+).
  • You are protected at Break Even (BE).
  • Price action shows clean momentum in your favor.

You're not weak for closing early on Fridays. You're wise.

4. Journaling & Performance

This is where you turn experience into wisdom.

What to Track:

  • Date & Pair, Entry type, HTF POI.
  • Result (TP/SL/BE) & Screenshot links.
  • What went right/wrong in 1 sentence.

"A trader who journals learns 3x faster than one who doesn’t."
