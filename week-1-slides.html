Lesson 1: Introduction to Forex

What is Forex?

The Foreign Exchange Market — where currencies are bought and sold.

It's the largest financial market in the world, with over $6 trillion traded daily!

Why Trade Forex?

  • High liquidity and 24/5 accessibility.
  • Opportunities to profit whether the market is going up or down.
  • Flexibility for part-time or full-time income.

Lesson 2: Currency Pairs & Sessions

Currency Pairs

Currencies are always traded in pairs, like a team.

Example: EUR/USD = 1.1000

Think of it like this: If you go to a foreign country, you exchange your money for their money. That exchange rate is what we trade in Forex.

Types of Pairs

  • Major Pairs: (e.g., EUR/USD, GBP/USD) - Most traded.
  • Minor Pairs: (e.g., EUR/GBP, GBP/JPY) - Less common.
  • Exotic Pairs: (e.g., USD/TRY, EUR/ZAR) - Least common, higher spread.

Market Sessions

The Forex market is open 24 hours a day, but activity changes depending on which global market is open.

  • Sydney & Tokyo: (Night/Morning Sessions) - Lower volume.
  • London: (The "Engine") - High volume and volatility.
  • New York: (The "Powerhouse") - High volume and volatility.

The best time to trade is often during the London–New York overlap (1 PM – 5 PM GMT), when the two biggest markets are active at the same time.

Lesson 3: Key Terminology

Pips

A Pip is the smallest unit of price movement.

Example: EUR/USD moves from 1.1000 to 1.1050. That's a 50-pip move.

Spreads

The difference between the Bid (buy) and Ask (sell) price. It's the fee you pay to your broker.

Leverage

It's like a loan from your broker. It allows you to control a large position with a small amount of capital.

Warning: Leverage can magnify profits, but it can also magnify losses.

Lesson 5: Risk & Reward

This is the most important lesson of the week.

The Golden Rule: 1:2 RR

For every trade, you must aim to make at least twice what you are risking.

Example: Risk $50 to make $100.

Think of it as a business. You wouldn't invest $100 to get back only $50. Trading is no different.

Your discipline with Risk-Reward is your true edge.
